Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is a Mars Rover?

A Mars Rover is a robotic vehicle that NASA sends to Mars to explore the surface, take photos, and gather scientific data about the planet's environment, rocks, and soil.

2. How many Mars Rovers are there?

As of now, NASA has sent five Mars Rovers: Sojourner, Spirit, Opportunity, Curiosity, and Perseverance.

3. What is the main goal of the Mars Rovers?

The primary goal is to study the Martian environment, including its geology, atmosphere, and potential for past or present life.

4. How do the Mars Rovers get power?

The Mars Rovers are powered either by solar panels (like Spirit and Opportunity) or by a nuclear-powered battery called an MMRTG (Multi-Mission Radioisotope Thermoelectric Generator), used by Curiosity and Perseverance.

5. What is the most recent Mars Rover?

The most recent Mars Rover is Perseverance, which landed on Mars in February 2021. It is exploring the Jezero Crater to search for signs of ancient life.

6. How long do Mars Rovers last?

Although Mars Rovers are designed for a mission duration of about 1-2 years, many have lasted much longer. For example, Opportunity lasted 15 years beyond its expected lifetime.

7. Why is Perseverance important?

Perseverance is important because it is searching for signs of ancient life, collecting samples for future return missions, and testing new technologies for human exploration of Mars.
