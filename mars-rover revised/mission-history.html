Mission History

Opportunity Rover

Mission Goals and Overview

Opportunity was part of NASA's Mars Exploration Rover mission, designed to search for and characterize rocks and soils indicating past water activity. Expected to operate for 90 Martian days, it functioned for nearly 15 years.

Launch and Landing Details

  • Launch Date: July 7, 2003
  • Launch Vehicle: Delta II rocket
  • Landing Date: January 25, 2004
  • Landing Site: Meridiani Planum

Major Discoveries or Accomplishments

  • First direct evidence of past liquid water on Mars.
  • Traveled over 28 miles—setting a Martian record.
  • Discovered hematite “blueberries” that form in water.

Key Technical or Design Features

  • Solar-powered, six-wheeled rover.
  • Equipped with Pancam, rock abrasion tool, and spectrometers.
  • Operated for 5,111 sols until a dust storm in 2018.

Curiosity Rover

Mission Goals and Overview

Curiosity is part of NASA's Mars Science Laboratory mission. Its primary goal is to assess whether Mars ever had conditions suitable for life. It continues to explore the Red Planet, studying its climate and geology.

Launch and Landing Details

  • Launch Date: November 26, 2011
  • Launch Vehicle: Atlas V rocket
  • Landing Date: August 6, 2012
  • Landing Site: Gale Crater

Major Discoveries or Accomplishments

  • Discovered ancient lakebeds and signs of long-lasting water.
  • Detected organic molecules and seasonal methane variation.
  • Provided high-res images of the Martian surface and sky.

Key Technical or Design Features

  • Nuclear-powered with extended mission duration.
  • Equipped with laser spectroscopy (ChemCam) and onboard lab tools.
  • Advanced suspension for rocky terrain and steep slopes.
