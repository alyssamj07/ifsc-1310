1. What is a Mars Rover?

A Mars Rover is a robotic vehicle that NASA sends to Mars to explore the surface, take photos, and gather scientific data about the planet's environment, rocks, and soil.

2. How many Mars Rovers are there?

NASA has launched five Mars Rovers: Sojourner, Spirit, Opportunity, Curiosity, and Perseverance.

3. What is the main goal of the Mars Rovers?

Their mission is to study the Martian environment, geology, atmosphere, and the potential for past or present life.

4. How do the Mars Rovers get power?

Some rovers use solar panels (e.g., Spirit and Opportunity), while others like Curiosity and Perseverance use nuclear power from an MMRTG (Multi-Mission Radioisotope Thermoelectric Generator).

5. What is the most recent Mars Rover?

Perseverance is the most recent rover. It landed on Mars in February 2021 and is exploring Jezero Crater.

7. Why is Perseverance important?
