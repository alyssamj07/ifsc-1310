The Mars Rover is a robotic vehicle designed to explore the surface of Mars. It captures images, collects soil samples, and searches for signs of life. Rovers are equipped with cameras, drills, and scientific instruments to help scientists understand the planet's geology and climate.

Mission Goals

The main goals of the Mars Rover missions include studying the climate, analyzing the geology, and preparing for future human exploration. Data collected by rovers helps NASA plan manned missions to Mars in the future.

Why Explore Mars?

Mars is the most Earth-like planet in our solar system. Exploring Mars could answer questions about the possibility of life beyond Earth and teach us about our planet's history and future.
